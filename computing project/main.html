At Elyon Christian School, students are expected to follow all school rules. If a student breaks the rules, there are certain steps and consequences to help them learn from their mistakes. This process is called Consequences and Mentoring.

Types of Offenses

  • Mild Offenses
  • Moderate Offenses
  • Severe Offenses

Examples of Mild Offenses:

  1. Coming late to class.
  2. Eating in class without permission.
  3. Using your phone during lessons.

When a student makes small mistakes (like being late or forgetting materials), teachers usually give a warning or a red slip. If the same behavior keeps happening, there will be more serious consequences, like community service or parent meetings.

Mentoring and Guidance

Students who get in trouble are not punished to be hurt but to help them grow. Teachers and mentors guide them to make better choices next time. This includes:

  • Talking with a mentor or counselor
  • Doing community service
  • Reporting regularly to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs
